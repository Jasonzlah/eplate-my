Lost or damaged JPJePlate: what should you do?

Practical steps if your electronic number plate is damaged, loose, unreadable, or missing.

If your JPJePlate is damaged or missing, do not ignore it. A number plate is part of your vehicle identification, so the safest move is to document the issue and contact the correct support channel quickly.

First steps

  • Take clear photos of the damaged or loose plate.
  • Keep your vehicle registration details nearby.
  • Contact the installer or official support channel that handled the order.
  • If the plate is stolen or missing, ask whether a police report is required for your case.

Do not modify the plate yourself

Avoid drilling, cutting, repainting, or altering the plate. DIY fixes can create compliance problems and may make the issue harder to resolve.

When to visit the workshop

If the plate is loose, cracked, or poorly mounted, the workshop can inspect the fitting and advise the next step. If a replacement is needed, the installer can guide you through the process and documents required.
